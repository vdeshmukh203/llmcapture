Core capabilities

Cryptographic chain

Every entry is SHA-256 hashed against the previous entry. Breaking any link is immediately detectable during verification.

Two-layer record

Captures raw keystrokes separately from rendered text — preserving what you typed versus what the AI received.

Exact timestamps

ISO 8601 timestamps on every entry and session event — start, capture, lock, finalize — with millisecond resolution.

Study manifest export

Exports a structured JSON forensic log plus a companion study manifest formatted for academic citation and legal disclosure.

How it works
1

Session anchoring

Each conversation thread gets a unique sessionId and a SHA-256 fingerprint computed from platform, URL, and thread key at creation time.

2

Entry chaining

Every captured turn — user prompt or AI response — is hashed as SHA-256(canonical_entry + previousHash). The first entry chains to GENESIS.

3

Streaming settlement

Assistant responses are captured only after text stabilises across two polling intervals — eliminating partial-stream artifacts from the record.

4

Verification on export

Export runs verifyChain() over all entries. The manifest records integrityStatus: "verified" or "tampered" alongside per-entry hash results.
